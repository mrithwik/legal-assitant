MATTER: John Kamau v Sarah Wanjiru — Breach of Agreement for Sale of Land

PARTIES
- Applicant: John Kamau, ID No. 12345678, of Nairobi County
- Respondent: Sarah Wanjiru, ID No. 87654321, of Kiambu County

BACKGROUND AND FACTS

On 15 March 2023, John Kamau (the Applicant) entered into a written Agreement for
Sale of Land with Sarah Wanjiru (the Respondent) for the purchase of land parcel
No. 123/456 situated in Kiambu County, measuring approximately 0.5 acres.

The agreed purchase price was Kenya Shillings Five Million (KES 5,000,000).

On the same date, 15 March 2023, the Applicant paid a deposit of Kenya Shillings
Five Hundred Thousand (KES 500,000), being 10% of the purchase price, by way of
M-Pesa transfer, reference number ABC123XYZ. The Respondent acknowledged receipt
of the deposit in writing.

The Agreement provided that the balance of KES 4,500,000 was to be paid within
90 days of signing, i.e., on or before 13 June 2023, upon which the Respondent
would execute all transfer documents and deliver vacant possession.

Upon payment of the deposit, the Respondent granted the Applicant possession of
the land, and the Applicant proceeded to fence the perimeter and construct a gate
at a cost of KES 120,000.

On 1 May 2023, the Applicant obtained a bank loan from Equity Bank Kenya Limited
and was ready to pay the balance. The Applicant notified the Respondent in writing
by letter dated 1 May 2023 that he was ready to complete the transaction.

The Respondent did not respond to the letter of 1 May 2023.

On 10 May 2023, the Respondent's advocate wrote to the Applicant's advocate
stating that the Respondent was purporting to resile from the Agreement on the
grounds that the agreed price was below market value and that the Agreement was
void for lack of independent legal advice.

On 20 May 2023, the Applicant's advocate wrote back disputing the Respondent's
position and demanding specific performance of the Agreement.

The Respondent has since refused and/or neglected to execute the transfer documents,
complete the transaction, or refund the deposit paid. The Respondent has also
demanded that the Applicant vacate the land.

The Applicant has continued to service his bank loan, incurring interest charges,
and has suffered loss and damage as a result of the Respondent's breach.

RELIEF SOUGHT

The Applicant seeks the following orders from the Honourable Court:

1. An order of specific performance compelling the Respondent to execute all
   necessary transfer documents for land parcel No. 123/456, Kiambu County
   in favour of the Applicant.

2. Alternatively, a refund of the deposit of KES 500,000 together with interest
   at court rates from 15 March 2023.

3. Damages for breach of contract including the cost of improvements made to the
   land (KES 120,000) and consequential losses arising from the bank loan.

4. Costs of this suit.

TIMELINE OF EVENTS

- 15 March 2023: Agreement for Sale signed; deposit of KES 500,000 paid;
                 possession granted to Applicant.
- 15 March 2023 onwards: Applicant fences land and constructs gate (KES 120,000).
- 1 May 2023: Applicant notifies Respondent in writing of readiness to complete.
- 10 May 2023: Respondent purports to resile from Agreement.
- 20 May 2023: Applicant demands specific performance.
- 13 June 2023: Contractual completion date (Respondent has not complied).
- July 2023: Respondent demands Applicant vacate the land.
